# Recommended Reading List

> **Owner:** Engineering Leadership | **Last Updated:** 2026-03-01

Books on this list are available via the company's O'Reilly Learning subscription or as physical copies in the office library. Use the Notion reading tracker to log progress and share notes.

---

## Engineering Fundamentals

| Title | Author | Why We Recommend It | Level |
|-------|--------|--------------------|----|
| *Designing Data-Intensive Applications* | Martin Kleppmann | The definitive guide to distributed systems, databases, and data engineering. Required reading before touching the data layer. | Advanced |
| *The Pragmatic Programmer* | Hunt & Thomas | Timeless principles for professional software development — applies regardless of language or role. | All levels |
| *A Philosophy of Software Design* | John Ousterhout | Challenges the "clean code" dogma with deeper thinking about complexity management. | Mid-level+ |
| *Release It!* | Michael Nygard | Stability patterns for production systems — circuit breakers, bulkheads, and timeouts come from here. | Mid-level+ |
| *Software Engineering at Google* | Winters, Manshreck, Wright | How Google thinks about engineering practices, testing, and code health at scale. | Mid-level+ |

---

## Systems & Architecture

| Title | Author | Why We Recommend It | Level |
|-------|--------|--------------------|----|
| *Building Microservices* | Sam Newman | The practical guide to service decomposition, communication patterns, and operational concerns. | Mid-level+ |
| *Fundamentals of Software Architecture* | Richards & Ford | Architecture styles, trade-off analysis, and how to make and communicate architectural decisions. | Mid-level+ |
| *System Design Interview (Vol 1 & 2)* | Alex Xu | Practical system design examples — good for interview prep and learning design vocabulary. | All levels |
| *Database Internals* | Alex Petrov | How databases actually work under the hood — storage engines, consensus, distributed transactions. | Advanced |

---

## Leadership & Process

| Title | Author | Why We Recommend It | Level |
|-------|--------|--------------------|----|
| *An Elegant Puzzle* | Will Larson | Engineering management frameworks from a seasoned engineering leader — practical and honest. | Managers |
| *The Manager's Path* | Camille Fournier | Career progression from engineer to CTO — the best technical management book written. | Everyone |
| *Accelerate* | Forsgren, Humble, Kim | The data behind the DORA metrics we use — explains why high-deployment frequency leads to better outcomes. | All levels |
| *Team Topologies* | Skelton & Pais | How to structure engineering teams around cognitive load and flow — informed our current team design. | Managers |
