# Market Entry Strategy

> **Owner:** Strategy Team | **Last Updated:** 2026-02-15

## Go-to-Market Motion

We use a **developer-led, enterprise-closed** GTM motion:

1. **Land** — developer discovers product through free tier, OSS SDK, or community
2. **Expand** — team adopts, usage grows organically within org
3. **Convert** — enterprise deal signed once critical mass of usage reached
4. **Grow** — expand to additional business units and use cases

This motion reduces CAC (no cold outreach required for land) while enabling large ACV deals at the convert stage.

## Target Customer Profile

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

| Attribute | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Company size** | 200–5,000 employees |
| **Industry** | Healthcare, Financial Services, Technology |
| **Technical maturity** | Has an engineering team (≥10 engineers) |
| **Pain profile** | Manual processes consuming > 20% of team capacity |
| **Budget authority** | VP Engineering or VP Operations can approve < $200K |
| **Buying trigger** | Compliance requirement, headcount freeze, or M&A integration |

### Negative ICP (Do Not Target)

- Companies with < 5 engineers (too small to integrate, high churn)
- Government agencies (procurement cycles too long, FedRAMP not yet achieved)
- Companies running on-premise only (our SaaS model doesn't fit)

## Competitive Displacement Strategy

### Against WorkflowCorp (Legacy Leader)

WorkflowCorp wins on brand recognition and ecosystem breadth. We win on:
- **Developer experience** — API-first, 10-minute time-to-value vs. their multi-day setup
- **AI capabilities** — native, not bolted-on
- **Pricing** — usage-based scales down for smaller orgs; WorkflowCorp minimum is $60K/yr

**Displacement playbook:**
1. Find a frustrated developer inside the account (usually in a team not using WorkflowCorp)
2. Win a small use case (30-day PoC, no IT involvement)
3. Expand within the team, then department
4. ROI case: calculate time saved × engineer hourly rate
5. Bring in economic buyer once usage exceeds $5K/month equivalent

### Against AutomateIQ (AI-Native Challenger)

AutomateIQ is our most similar competitor. We win on:
- **Reliability** — their p99 latency is 2.3× ours (public benchmark data)
- **Enterprise features** — RBAC, audit logs, SSO (they lack all three)
- **Security posture** — SOC2 Type II certified; they are not

**Head-to-head messaging:** "AutomateIQ is great for prototypes. We're built for production."
