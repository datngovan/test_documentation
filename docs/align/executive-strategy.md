# Board-Level Strategic Plan FY2026

> **Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL — Board & C-Suite Only | **Prepared by:** Office of the CEO | **Date:** 2026-01-15

## Strategic Direction

FY2026 represents a pivotal year for the company. After three years of product-led growth, we are transitioning to an enterprise-first go-to-market motion while preserving our developer community as the primary top-of-funnel channel. The board has approved the following strategic pivots effective Q1 2026.

### Strategic Pivot 1: AI-Native Platform Positioning

We will reposition from "workflow automation platform" to "AI-native orchestration platform." This is not a branding exercise — it requires genuine product investment to ensure AI capabilities are embedded in every layer of the product, not bolted on as a feature.

**Board Directive:** Allocate 40% of engineering capacity to AI-related initiatives in FY2026 (up from 15% in FY2025). This may require pausing lower-priority roadmap items.

### Strategic Pivot 2: Vertical-First Sales Strategy

Horizontal platform sales have reached diminishing returns in our current market position. We will build vertical-specific packages for three target industries:

| Vertical | Target ARR (FY2026) | Investment Required | Expected CAC | Strategic Rationale |
|----------|---------------------|--------------------|--------------|--------------------|
| Healthcare | $14M | $2.8M | $12,000 | Highest growth rate (24%), regulatory moat, strong existing foothold |
| Financial Services | $10M | $2.2M | $18,000 | Large deal sizes ($400K+ ACV), compliance requirements favor established platforms |
| Manufacturing | $6M | $1.4M | $15,000 | IoT/edge workflow opportunity, less competition, sticky use cases |

### Strategic Pivot 3: European Expansion

European market entry is a board-level priority for H2 2026. The GDPR expertise we have built for compliance features gives us a differentiation angle that US-first competitors lack.

**Key Decision:** We will establish a legal entity in Ireland (EU headquarters) and hire a VP of EMEA Sales by Q3 2026. Initial headcount plan: 8 (3 sales, 2 solutions engineering, 2 customer success, 1 country manager).

## M&A Strategy

### Acquisition Targets

The board has approved exploration of the following acquisition targets to accelerate our strategic pivots:

| Target | Estimated Valuation | Strategic Fit | Priority | Status |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|----------|--------|
| DataWeave Analytics | $25-35M | AI/ML talent acquisition + healthcare NLP models | High | LOI stage — due diligence in progress |
| FlowSync (open-source) | $8-12M | Developer community (18K GitHub stars) + Kubernetes-native workflow engine | High | Preliminary conversations via CEO-to-CEO |
| ComplianceBot | $15-20M | Automated compliance checking for fintech workflows | Medium | Identified — no contact yet |
| EdgeRunner | $5-8M | Edge computing runtime for IoT workflows | Low | Watch list — revisit in Q3 |

**Budget:** Board has approved up to $45M for M&A in FY2026, funded through existing cash reserves ($28M) and a potential debt facility ($20M credit line from SVB, term sheet under review).

### Competitive Acquisition Defense

Intelligence indicates that WorkflowCorp (our largest competitor) is actively acquiring smaller players in our space. Their acquisition of DataPipe in Q4 2025 signals a move into data orchestration. We should expect them to target:

- Open-source workflow tools (to neutralize the community-led adoption channel)
- Vertical-specific automation companies (to build industry expertise we currently lead in)
- AI startups with workflow-adjacent capabilities

**Board Directive:** If WorkflowCorp makes a move on FlowSync, we should be prepared to make a preemptive offer within 2 weeks. Legal and finance should have a term sheet template ready.
