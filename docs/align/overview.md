# Align Phase Overview

The **Align** phase is the foundation of the APEI framework. Before any planning begins, teams must establish a shared understanding of purpose, direction, and priorities. Alignment ensures that every subsequent action serves the organization's strategic intent.

## What Is Alignment?

Alignment is the process of ensuring that all stakeholders share a common understanding of:

- **Vision**: Where we want to be in the long term
- **Mission**: Why we exist and what value we deliver
- **Strategic priorities**: What matters most right now
- **Constraints**: What boundaries and limitations shape our decisions

Without alignment, teams risk building the right thing for the wrong reason, or the wrong thing entirely.

## Alignment Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Clarity over consensus | It is better to have a clear direction that some disagree with than a vague direction everyone accepts |
| Written over verbal | Alignment artifacts must be documented, not just discussed |
| Inclusive input, decisive output | Gather perspectives broadly, but let decision-makers decide |
| Revisit regularly | Alignment is not a one-time event; revisit quarterly at minimum |
| Measure understanding | Test alignment by asking team members to articulate the strategy independently |

## Key Questions to Answer

Before moving to the Plan phase, the team should be able to answer these questions confidently:

1. **Why does this initiative exist?** What problem are we solving, and for whom?
2. **What does success look like?** Can we describe the desired end state concretely?
3. **What are our constraints?** Budget, timeline, technology, regulatory, or team capacity limits?
4. **Who are our stakeholders?** Who needs to be informed, consulted, or approve decisions?
5. **What are the risks?** What could prevent us from succeeding, and how likely are those risks?
6. **How does this connect to the broader strategy?** Where does this fit in the organizational roadmap?

## Alignment Artifacts

At the end of the Align phase, you should have:

- [ ] A signed-off **Vision Statement**
- [ ] A documented **Mission Statement**
- [ ] A prioritized list of **Strategic Objectives**
- [ ] A **Stakeholder Map** with roles and responsibilities
- [ ] An **Alignment Score** from the team survey (target: 80%+ agreement)

## The Alignment Workshop

We recommend running a structured alignment workshop at the start of each quarter. The format:

```
Duration: 2-3 hours
Attendees: All team leads + key stakeholders

Agenda:
1. Review previous quarter outcomes (20 min)
2. Present strategic context and market signals (30 min)
3. Breakout: Define top 3 priorities per team (30 min)
4. Converge: Map priorities to strategic objectives (30 min)
5. Identify dependencies and conflicts (20 min)
6. Commit: Sign off on alignment document (10 min)
```

## Moving to Plan

The Align phase is complete when:

- All key stakeholders have reviewed and approved the alignment document
- The team can articulate the top three priorities without referring to notes
- Dependencies between teams are identified and acknowledged
- Risk register is populated with at least the top five risks

> **Tip**: If you find yourself revisiting alignment questions during the Plan phase, it is a signal that the Align phase was not thorough enough. Do not hesitate to loop back.
